My Love and Commitment to Good Coffee

Why coffee?

My love for coffee began like mosts - keeping me awake during my studies!

But my enjoyment for coffee didn't end there. I began my experience with coffee brewing espresso. Fortunately, I have always worked with independent coffee companies which kept me open to a range of different beans from a range of locations and farmers. This fed my curiosity for getting more flavor out of the beans I had been working with and opened up the world of other brewing methods. Coming from an english household I began with a cafetiere (French press) and soon purchased my own V60. And so began my specialty coffee journey.

Since then I have had barista, brewing and sensory training with Origin Coffee, Allpress Espresso and SCA (Specialist Coffee Association), and over 3 years onsite barista experience.

Certificates

SCA 'CSP Barista Skills Foundation'

Barista Skills teaches the essentials practical skills needed behind the espresso bar such as how to set your grinder, make espresso, foam and texture milk for cappuccinos, and create latte art as well as an exploration of health and safety issues, customer service protocols, and basic business practices.
The Barista Skills Foundation course allows learners to gain an introductory understanding of the coffee itself as well as foundational skills required to set a grinder, make espressos, foam and texture milk and latte art techniques as per SCA quality standards, while implementing health and safety practices and customer service. Practical learning objectives and activities prepare the learner to conduct key foundational tasks of a barista. A written exam tests theoretical knowledge based on Foundation course learning objectives.

SCA 'CSP Brewing Skills Foundation'

The Brewing courses cover numerous brewing coffee methods as well as the brewing variables that affect quality. Hands on learning is emphasized with a focus on grind profiles, brewing methods, coffee strength measurements, and coffee extraction charting.
The Brewing Foundation course introduces the learner to the different methods of brewing coffee. The learner will receive theoretical and practical hands-on instruction for a range of devices including automatic and manual gravity brewers, as well as other commonly used brewers within their local culture. Practical learning objectives and activities prepare the learner to produce a tasty brew based on an understanding of the essential brewing elements and an analysis of their brew results. A written exam tests theoretical knowledge based on Foundation course learning objectives.
