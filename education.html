My Educational Style

My educational practice is tailored to each individual's goals, whether they aim for a professional career, a hobby, or emotional well-being. I like to offer both online and in-person lessons (in the Cambridge area), with flexible durations from 30 minutes to 2 hours. My teaching style combines improvisation, exploration, and imitation, fostering creativity and problem-solving through hands-on experience with software such as Ableton Live, Logic Pro, ProTools, and Cubase. My lessons cover music theory, DAW practice, composition, mixing, and mastering, with all materials provided digitally.

I emphasise a supportive and inclusive environment, drawing on my SEND (Special Educational Needs and Disabilities) teaching experience to ensure all students feel empowered. Assessments are personalised and discussed from the first session, avoiding rigid structures that might hinder progress. With a BA(Hons) in Creative Musicianship; a Level 3 Diploma in Music Performance; and extensive experience as a freelance musician, I bring both technical expertise and real-world insights to my teaching. In summary, my lessons are designed to help you achieve your musical goals in a dynamic and engaging way.

In addition to a strong focus on music production, my lessons also incorporate insights from my Level 2 Counselling Skills Diploma, ensuring a supportive and empathetic learning environment. I am adept at understanding and addressing the emotional aspects of learning, which is crucial for building confidence and fostering a positive atmosphere. My freelance experience extends beyond music to include graphic design and video direction, allowing me to teach students about the visual and promotional aspects of their music careers. This holistic approach prepares students for the multifaceted demands of the modern music industry.
